Algorithmic Trading Course

AI & Algorithmic Trading — Volumes I–III + User Manual

A three-volume, governance-first collection with a dedicated user manual and twenty-five executable Colab notebooks. This course teaches algorithmic trading as a disciplined engineering system: data logic, causality, honest backtesting, strategy families, machine learning, portfolios, execution realism, and the artifacts required to make results auditable and trustworthy.

Pedagogical Pillars

The collection is designed to prevent the most expensive failure modes in quantitative finance: look-ahead bias, non-reproducible results, and “backtest theater.” The goal is to teach methods that survive scrutiny.

Governance-First Thinking

Research is treated as an auditable process. Data fingerprints, run manifests, and explicit decision timing are core learning tools, not bureaucracy.

Time as a Structural Constraint

The course enforces causality and chronological integrity, eliminating look-ahead bias and misleading validation practices.

Synthetic Data as Ground Truth

Strategies are validated on controlled synthetic processes before touching real markets, separating code correctness from market noise.

Executable Understanding

Every chapter has a corresponding Colab notebook, allowing readers to move directly from concept to implementation.
