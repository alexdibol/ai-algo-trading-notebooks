What this collection is (and why it’s different)

Most trading material teaches you how to produce a backtest plot. This collection teaches you how to build a research laboratory and a deployment-ready workflow — with explicit handling of the two things that destroy most quantitative work: time leakage and non-reproducibility.

The structure is deliberate. You validate logic on synthetic data first (where ground truth is known), then graduate to realistic assumptions and evaluation. You learn how strategies fail, why they fail, and what minimum standards must exist before you claim performance.

  • Governance-first philosophy: artifacts, manifests, and auditability are treated as core research tools, not bureaucracy.
  • Synthetic data as “truth serum”: separate code correctness from market noise before you touch real data.
  • Time awareness as a hard constraint: evaluation respects chronology and decision timing, avoiding look-ahead bias.
  • Executable pedagogy: each chapter has a Colab notebook, reducing friction from concept to implementation.
